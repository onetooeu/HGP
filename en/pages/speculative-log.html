Speculative Log (science‑fantasy)

Important: This is a creative / science‑fantasy appendix written “as if”. It is not a claim about reality. It exists to practice hypothesis design and falsification.

Log 001 — “Gravitational Compression Analogy”

Imagine the “field” is not an energy source but a metric of ordering. We explore a purely hypothetical scenario: if an informational gradient were coupled to geometry, what measurement methodology would be required to falsify our own claim?

  • Propose a measurable quantity (with units) and a null hypothesis.
  • Define what a “positive signal” would mean and what artifacts can mimic it.
  • Make a conservative sensitivity estimate: what is the detection limit under noise?

Log 002 — “The Zero‑Baseline Rule”

Every speculative curve must be paired with a zero baseline (classical expectation). If the model cannot explain why baseline wouldn’t be zero, the model fails.

How to use it

  1. Do not use the log for marketing or fundraising.
  2. For each claim, add: (a) units, (b) energy source, (c) falsification test.
  3. If it can’t be tested, label it non‑testable and archive it.
