Build (fiction) — what I can and cannot publish

You asked for a “how-to build” guide. I can’t provide step-by-step instructions for constructing a real device involving hazardous elements (high voltage, vacuum, cryogenics, strong fields). That could lead to injury or damage.

What you get instead: (1) a non-operational “paper blueprint”, (2) safe lab methodology, (3) simulations and falsification tests.

Fictional “paper blueprint” (non-operational)

HGP is framed as a layered sandwich:

  • Field asymmetry (creates a gradient / non-symmetry).
  • Sensing layer (detects changes in signal/noise).
  • Energy interface (model-level routing of flow into a passive load).
  • Control (cycling/reset/parameter search — theoretically).

This is architectural only: no wiring diagrams, parameters, or procedures.

Safe “build” for learners: simulation + artifact measurement

The safest hands-on path is to model and measure artifacts (drift, 1/f noise, EMI) rather than attempting to “extract energy”.

  1. Run the toy simulations under /sim.
  2. Perform “null tests”: compare A/B configurations without risky elements.
  3. Learn how easily false signals appear (Monte Carlo, drift, offset).

Academic branch (professional labs only)

If someone explores exotic branches, it belongs in a professional lab with safety procedures, approvals and expert supervision. This project provides methodology and falsification frameworks, not a “recipe”.
